We're a small, computationally-leaning lab built around close mentorship and shared ownership of projects. Lab members come from biology, computer science, physics, and engineering backgrounds — what unifies us is a willingness to develop tools when the tools don't exist, and to work on biology that matters.

## Principal Investigator

**Jin Chen, Ph.D.** — Principal Investigator, Altos Labs · Bay Area Institute of Science
Assistant Professor, Department of Pharmacology · Cecil H. and Ida Green Center · Harold C. Simmons Comprehensive Cancer Center · University of Texas Southwestern Medical Center

Jin leads the Chen Lab at Altos Labs, where he and his team work at the intersection of functional genomics, the dark proteome, and rejuvenation biology. He trained as a physicist before crossing into biology — B.S. and M.S. in Applied Physics and Biomedical Engineering at Yale (2010), and a Ph.D. in Applied Physics (Biophysics) at Stanford in 2015 with Joseph Puglisi, where he developed single-molecule technologies for studying ribosome translation in real time. He also holds an M.S. in Electrical Engineering (Machine Learning) from Stanford, completed in parallel. As a postdoctoral fellow at UCSF (2016–2020) with Jonathan Weissman, Jin systematically uncovered and characterized microproteins encoded by non-canonical open reading frames and co-developed CRISPRoff/on for inheritable epigenetic silencing. He started his independent lab as an Assistant Professor at UT Southwestern Medical Center in 2020 — an appointment he maintains today — and joined Altos Labs as a Principal Investigator in 2022 to lead his rejuvenation and cell-state biology program at scale.

## Joining the lab

We're actively recruiting postdoctoral fellows and staff scientists interested in any of our four research directions. Strong candidates typically have a background in one or more of: molecular biology, RNA biology, functional genomics, single-cell / spatial methods, CRISPR perturbation screens, or quantitative and computational biology — and an appetite to cross into the others.

To apply, email Jin ([jchen@altoslabs.com](mailto:jchen@altoslabs.com)) with:

1. A CV
2. A one-page research statement — what you'd want to work on, not just what you have worked on
3. Contact information for three references

We read every application.
