What’s happening in the lab

Papers, talks, personnel moves, and other things worth writing down.

May 19, 2026

A new site, and a sharper focus. Since moving to Altos Labs in 2022, our research has expanded from translational control into the broader question of how cell states are set, maintained, and reversed — with microproteins and high-throughput perturbation as our two main levers. The website is finally caught up with where the lab actually is.

Mar 5, 2026

Jin gave an invited talk at Perturb2026 in Vienna — Large-scale Perturb-seq in primary human disease models: disease-reversal programs and virtual cell perturbation mapping. First full public airing of the lab’s virtual-cell effort.

Jan 30, 2026

Our paper on the functional landscape of non-canonical open reading frames in coordinating cell fate is out in Molecular Cell. Congratulations to Kaiyuan and the team — this is the first flagship paper from the Altos-era lab.

Nov 4, 2025

Yi-Hsuan Lo’s paper on large-scale CRISPR screening in primary human 3D gastric organoids is out in Nature Communications — a long-running collaboration with the Kuo lab at Stanford.

Jun 15, 2025

Jin gave an invited talk at the Keystone Symposium "Microproteins: from Discovery to Applications" — Coding or non-coding? Illuminating the "dark matter" of the proteome.
